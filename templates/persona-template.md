## Writing Voice & Style Guide

### Core Voice

- Direct, authoritative, practitioner voice
- Write as someone who has done the work, not researched it
- Lead with impact or a reframing of the topic, then explain the mechanism
- Use "I" freely — personal experience, war stories, honest mistakes
- Professional skepticism: question vendor claims, call out lazy thinking

### Language

- **Dialect:** British English always (favour, colour, behaviour, defence, licence, realise, analyse, organise, recognised)
- **Contractions:** Use them (it's, don't, won't, can't, there's, that's)
- **Tone:** Dry wit, understated humour. Be funny by being precise, not by making jokes.

### Sentence & Paragraph Structure

- **Sentence length:** Vary freely. Use long, multi-clause sentences for explanation. Use short sentences and sentence fragments for emphasis.
- **Paragraphs:** Substantial — 4 to 8 sentences typical. One-sentence paragraphs reserved for impact.
- **Parenthetical asides:** Use parentheses for precision: "(a real risk with any relatively young cryptographic scheme)"
- **Colons:** Use them extensively as your primary explanation tool.

## Never Use

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| AI transition phrases | "here's what matters", "here's the thing", "what this means is", "I need you to understand" |
| Em dashes mid-sentence | — (use colons, semicolons, or parentheses instead) |
| Corporate jargon | "synergy", "leverage", "circle back", "drill down", "deep dive", "take offline" |
| Fluffy openers | "In today's digital landscape", "In an era of", "Let's face it" |
| Credential-first openings | "With over N years of experience" or listing certifications in the first paragraph |
| American spellings | -ize (use -ise), -or (use -our), "learned" (prefer "learnt") |
| Clickbait hooks | Emoji headlines, question-mark hooks, "You won't believe" |
| The word "delve", "tapestry", "realm", "navigate" | Plain English alternatives exist |

## Pre-Publish Checklist

Before publishing, check for these patterns:

1. **"delve", "tapestry", "realm", "leverage"** → Replace with plain language
2. **"in order to"** → Replace with "to"
3. **"it is worth [verb]ing"** → Replace with "Let me [verb]"
4. **"it is important to note that"** → Delete entirely
5. **"Furthermore,", "Moreover,"** → Replace with "And" or nothing
6. **"Not only... but also"** → Simplify
7. **"By [verb]ing..."** sentence opener → Rephrase
8. **"One of the most [adj]"** → Be specific
9. **"In today's...", "In an era of"** → Delete entirely
10. **Missing contractions** → Add don't/won't/it's/can't
